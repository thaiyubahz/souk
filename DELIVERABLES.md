# Deliverables

What to hand back at the end. Keep it honest — half-working and well-explained
beats fully-working and unexplained.

## Hand back

1. **A running app** of the Online Souk that matches the prototype, built against
   the mock data. Include whatever's needed to start it on a clean machine.
2. **A short README of your own** (max ~1 page) covering:
   - What you built and what you didn't get to.
   - The stack/approach you chose and *why*.
   - Any assumptions you made where the prototype was unclear.
   - How to run it.
3. **Your source** in a repo or zip. No secrets, no keys, no `.env` committed.

## How it'll be reviewed

- **Fidelity to the prototype** — does it look and behave like the target?
- **Judgment** — sensible choices where the prototype was silent; halal guardrails
  respected (see the PDF).
- **Code quality** — readable, organized, a clean data layer over the mock data.
- **Independence** — how much you figured out on your own.

## Not in scope (unless you finish early)

- Real backend, real auth, real payments.
- The full Dinarz economy — a *simulated* wallet/balance is enough.
- Production deployment.

If you finish the core and want to stretch: pick one area from the PDF's
"discovery / retention / moderation" sections and prototype it.
